# Implementation Guide

## Purpose

This guide provides a practical approach for implementing the NovaTide Logistics Vendor Risk Scoring Framework within an organization's Third-Party Risk Management (TPRM) program. It is intended for security, procurement, compliance, and business stakeholders responsible for vendor governance.

---

# Implementation Objectives

The framework is designed to help organizations:

- Establish a repeatable vendor assessment process.
- Prioritize vendor oversight based on business risk.
- Reduce reliance on annual questionnaire-only assessments.
- Introduce continuous monitoring for critical vendors.
- Improve audit readiness through documented assessment methodology.

---

# Implementation Phases

## Phase 1 – Build the Vendor Inventory

Create a centralized inventory of all third-party vendors.

Each vendor record should include:

- Vendor information
- Business capability supported
- Business owner
- Technology owner
- Vendor tier
- Assessment dates
- Contract renewal dates

**Deliverable**

- Completed Vendor Inventory worksheet

---

## Phase 2 – Classify Vendor Criticality

Assign each vendor to one of four tiers.

| Tier | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Critical | Supports mission-critical business operations or has access to regulated or sensitive information. |
| High | Important operational dependency with elevated business impact. |
| Medium | Moderate operational dependency with manageable business impact. |
| Low | Limited business dependency and minimal security exposure. |

Vendor tier determines the assessment frequency and monitoring requirements.

---

## Phase 3 – Perform the Risk Assessment

Evaluate each vendor across five assessment domains.

| Assessment Domain | Weight |
|------------------|-------:|
| Governance & Compliance | 25% |
| Security Posture | 25% |
| Business Dependency | 20% |
| Continuous Monitoring | 20% |
| Concentration & Resilience | 10% |

The weighted scores produce the vendor's Composite Risk Score.

---

## Phase 4 – Determine Monitoring Requirements

Apply monitoring activities based on vendor tier.

| Vendor Tier | Monitoring Approach |
|-------------|--------------------|
| Critical | Continuous monitoring with quarterly review |
| High | Continuous monitoring with semi-annual review |
| Medium | Monthly monitoring with annual reassessment |
| Low | Annual reassessment with exception-based monitoring |

---

## Phase 5 – Track Assessment Half-Life

Assessment results become less reliable over time.

The framework assigns an Assessment Half-Life to each vendor tier.

| Vendor Tier | Assessment Half-Life |
|-------------|--------------------:|
| Critical | 90 Days |
| High | 180 Days |
| Medium | 365 Days |
| Low | 730 Days |

When the Half-Life expires, the vendor should be reassessed or monitoring evidence should be reviewed before relying on the previous assessment.

---

## Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Procurement | Initiates vendor onboarding and coordinates assessments. |
| Business Owner | Confirms operational dependency and business impact. |
| Technology Owner | Validates technical controls and service integration. |
| Information Security | Performs security assessment and monitoring. |
| Risk & Compliance | Reviews composite scores and approves vendor risk decisions. |

---

## Success Criteria

Implementation is considered successful when:

- All vendors are inventoried.
- Every vendor has an assigned tier.
- Composite scores are calculated consistently.
- Assessment Half-Life is tracked.
- Continuous monitoring is enabled for Critical and High vendors.
- Vendor reviews follow documented assessment schedules.

---

## Scope

This implementation guide supports the fictional NovaTide Logistics Enterprise Simulation and is intended for educational and portfolio purposes. Organizations should adapt the framework to their regulatory requirements, risk appetite, and governance processes.
